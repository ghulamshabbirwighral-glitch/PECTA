Active and Passive Voice

In English grammar, verbs have two voices: Active Voice and Passive Voice. Voice shows whether the subject of a sentence is performing the action or receiving the action.

💡 Basic Concept:

  • Active Voice: The subject does the action. (e.g., Ali wrote a letter.)
  • Passive Voice: The subject receives the action. (e.g., A letter was written by Ali.)

Formula / Structure

Voice Structure
Active Subject + Verb + Object
Passive Object + Be Verb (is/am/are/was/were etc.) + V3 (3rd Form) + by + Subject

Rules for Changing Voice

  1. The object of the active sentence becomes the subject of the passive sentence.
  2. Always use the 3rd form of the verb (Past Participle) in passive voice.
  3. Use the word "by" before the subject in the passive voice.
  4. Pronouns change their forms (He ➔ Him, She ➔ Her, I ➔ Me, They ➔ Them, We ➔ Us).

Examples Across Tenses

Tense Active Voice Passive Voice
Present Simple She cleans the room. The room is cleaned by her.
Past Simple She cleaned the room. The room was cleaned by her.
Future Simple She will clean the room. The room will be cleaned by her.
Present Continuous She is cleaning the room. The room is being cleaned by her.
