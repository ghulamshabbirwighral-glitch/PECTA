Direct and Indirect Speech

Direct and Indirect Speech (also known as Narration) is used to tell what someone else has said.

💡 Definitions:

  • Direct Speech: Repeating the exact words spoken by a person, using quotation marks (" ").
    Example: He said, "I am happy."
  • Indirect Speech: Reporting what someone said in our own words, without quotation marks.
    Example: He said that he was happy.

How Tenses Change

If the reporting verb is in the past tense (e.g., He said), the tense inside the inverted commas changes as follows:

Direct Speech Indirect Speech
Present Simple (write) Past Simple (wrote)
Present Continuous (is writing) Past Continuous (was writing)
Present Perfect (has written) Past Perfect (had written)
Past Simple (wrote) Past Perfect (had written)
Will / Shall Would / Should
Can / May Could / Might

Changes in Time and Place Words

Direct Speech Indirect Speech
This That
Here There
Today That day
Tomorrow The next day / The following day
Yesterday The day before / The previous day

Example Sentences

  • Direct: She said, "I am cooking dinner."
  • Indirect: She said that she was cooking dinner.

  • Direct: They said, "We have won the match."
  • Indirect: They said that they had won the match.
